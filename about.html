Who We Are

The Ilocano Spirit

Ilocos Norte sits at the northwestern tip of Luzon Island, cradled between the Luzon Strait to the north and the South China Sea to the west. It is a land of striking contrasts — rugged coastlines and gentle sand, colonial churches and modern wind farms, ancient tradition and forward-looking ambition.

The province is one of the oldest settled regions in the Philippines. Long before Spanish colonization, Ilocano communities traded along coastal routes and cultivated the land with remarkable industry. That spirit of enterprise and resilience became the defining characteristic of the Ilocano people — a reputation that endures to this day.

Today, Ilocos Norte is a premier tourism destination — celebrated for its UNESCO Baroque churches, the iconic Bangui Windmills, pristine beaches, sand dunes, and a cuisine that is distinctly, boldly Ilocano.

Timeline

A Rich History

🏺
Pre-Colonial Era

Ancient Ilocandia

Before Spanish contact, Ilocano communities had established thriving coastal settlements and trade networks across the northwestern coast of Luzon.

⛪
1572 — Spanish Era

Colonial Period

Spanish colonizers arrived and established missions. The construction of Baroque churches — including the iconic Paoay Church — began, blending Spanish and Ilocano architectural traditions.

🇵🇭
1898 — Independence

Philippine Republic

Ilocos Norte became a province of the independent Philippine Republic. The region played a significant role in the struggle for national freedom.

🌬️
Modern Era

Wind & Tourism

The construction of the Bangui Windmills in 2005 transformed the province's skyline and energy landscape. UNESCO recognition of Paoay Church brought international attention.

Location

Where Is Ilocos Norte?

Ilocos Norte occupies the northwestern tip of Luzon, approximately 480 km north of Manila. It is bordered by Cagayan and Abra to the east, Ilocos Sur to the south, and the South China Sea and Luzon Strait to the west and north.

By Air
Laoag International Airport (LAO) — ~1 hour from Manila
By Bus
Overnight buses from Pasay / Cubao terminals — 9–11 hours
By Car
Via NLEX–SCTEX and the Ilocos coastal highway — ~7–8 hours
