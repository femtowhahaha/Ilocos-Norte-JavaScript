Coastal Gems

Paradise Beaches

Ilocos Norte's coastline stretches for kilometers of raw, unspoiled beauty along the South China Sea and Luzon Strait.

Top Beach · Pagudpud

Saud Beach

The crown jewel of Ilocos Norte's coastline. Powdery white sand, warm crystal waters, and a serene atmosphere make this the most beloved beach in the entire northern Philippines. Perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and sunset-watching.

Full Details
Hidden Gem · Pagudpud

Blue Lagoon

A breathtaking cove of impossibly clear turquoise water hemmed between rocky cliffs and lush forest. Less crowded than Saud and arguably more dramatic — a true hidden paradise.

Full Details
Coastal · Bangui

Bangui Bay

A dramatic black-sand bay beneath 20 towering wind turbines. The contrast of crashing waves, white turbines, and dark sand is unforgettable and utterly unique.

Full Details
Scenic Drive · Pagudpud

Patapat Viaduct

A breathtaking coastal viaduct hugging sheer cliffs above the South China Sea — one of the most scenic coastal drives in Southeast Asia. The views are extraordinary.

Full Details
Waterfall · Pagudpud

Kabigan Falls

A stunning 35-meter waterfall cascading into a clear natural pool amid lush rainforest. Reached via a scenic 30-minute trek through the verdant Ilocos Norte countryside.

Beach Tips for Visitors

Best Season

November to May is the dry season — ideal for beach activities with calm seas and sunny skies. Avoid typhoon season (June–October) for beach trips.

Safety Note

Always check sea conditions before swimming. Some coves have strong currents. Follow local lifeguard advisories and swim only in designated areas.

Eco Tips

Please take your trash home, avoid touching corals when snorkeling, and support local beach vendors. Help keep Ilocos Norte's coastlines pristine.
