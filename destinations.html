UNESCO Heritage · Paoay

Paoay Church

A UNESCO World Heritage Site and masterpiece of Earthquake Baroque architecture, built in 1704. Its massive coral stone buttresses are unlike any structure in the Philippines.

Discover More
Landmark · Bangui

Bangui Windmills

20 towering wind turbines stretching along Bangui Bay's black-sand shore — a hypnotic, surreal, and entirely unique Philippine landscape.

Discover More
Rock Formation · Burgos

Kapurpurawan Rock Formation

Dazzling white rock formations carved by wind and sea over millennia. Only accessible at low tide — a surreal, otherworldly landscape on the northern coast.

Discover More
Beach · Pagudpud

Saud Beach

Powdery white sand, crystal-blue waters, and gentle waves. Known as the "Boracay of the North," Saud Beach is Ilocos Norte's most celebrated coastal escape.

Discover More
Lighthouse · Burgos

Cape Bojeador Lighthouse

Built in 1892, this octagonal brick lighthouse perches on a hill above the South China Sea, offering sweeping panoramic views of the northern Philippine coast.

Discover More
Adventure · Laoag

La Paz Sand Dunes

A vast desert-like expanse of sand dunes rising near Laoag City — the largest sand dunes in the Philippines, perfect for 4x4 rides and sandboarding.

Discover More
Lagoon · Pagudpud

Blue Lagoon

A serene cove with impossibly clear turquoise waters tucked between dramatic coastal cliffs. One of the most scenic swimming spots in Northern Luzon.

Discover More
Capital · Laoag

Laoag City

The proud capital of Ilocos Norte — a city where colonial-era bell towers, modern malls, the famous sand dunes, and the warmth of Ilocano hospitality converge.

Rock Formation · Adams

Timmangtang Rock

A dramatic rock formation rising from the mountains of Adams — accessible via a thrilling hike through verdant forests and swift rivers. A true adventurer's reward.
