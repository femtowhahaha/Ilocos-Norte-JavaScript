Location: Laoag City Month Celebrated: February

Festival 2

Kannawidan Ylocos Festival

Location: Ilocos Norte Provincial Capitol, Laoag City Month Celebrated: January

Kannawidan Ylocos Festival celebrates the inherited traditions of the Ilocano people. It highlights the culture that families and communities continue to preserve through food, craft, performance, agriculture, and public celebration.

Visitors can expect cultural shows, local product exhibits, weaving and craft displays, traditional games, music, dances, food booths, and performances from different towns. It is one of the best events for seeing Ilocos Norte as a living culture, not only as a collection of tourist spots.

Required Short Description

A January heritage festival in Laoag City that showcases Ilocano traditions, crafts, food, performances, and community identity.

Festival 3

Paoay Lakbay-Tikim

Location: Paoay Month Celebrated: April

Paoay Lakbay-Tikim connects food tourism with the town's strong heritage identity. It gives visitors a reason to slow down after seeing Paoay Church and experience the flavors, stories, and everyday hospitality of the community.

Local cooks and vendors can feature Ilocano favorites such as empanada, longganisa, bagnet, pinakbet, rice cakes, and other town specialties. The event supports small food businesses while helping tourists understand how food, faith, architecture, and family traditions meet in Paoay.

Required Short Description

An April food and heritage event in Paoay featuring Ilocano dishes, local products, cultural activities, and town-based tourism.

Festival Visitor Notes

How to Experience Festival Culture

Arrive Early

Main streets and plaza areas can become crowded during parades and evening programs. Arriving early gives better views and more time to explore food stalls.

Support Local Makers

Festival booths often feature woven goods, crafts, farm products, snacks, and souvenirs from local communities. Buying locally helps sustain the traditions being presented.

Respect Community Spaces

Many activities use church grounds, civic spaces, schools, and town plazas. Ask before taking close-up photos of performers, elders, or private booths.
