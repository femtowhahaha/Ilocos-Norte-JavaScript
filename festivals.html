Signature Festival

Pamulinawen Festival

The grandest celebration in Ilocos Norte, held every February in Laoag City. The Pamulinawen Festival is named after the famous Ilocano folk song of the same name — a timeless ballad about a man pleading with his beloved not to have a heart of stone.

The festival features elaborate street parades, traditional Ilocano performances, beauty pageants, cultural nights, and trade fairs. It is the culmination of the week-long Laoag City Charter Day anniversary celebration — drawing visitors from across the Philippines and beyond.

Festival Calendar

January

Laoag City Fiesta

The annual patronal feast of Saint William — mass, procession, cultural shows, and community events throughout the capital city.

February

Pamulinawen Festival

Laoag City's grandest cultural celebration — street dancing, parades, beauty pageants, and the heart of Ilocano pride on full display.

April

Paoay Lakbay-Tikim

A food and heritage festival in Paoay showcasing traditional Ilocano cuisine, handcrafts, and the cultural richness of the Paoay municipality.

October

Bangui Wind Festival

Celebrating the iconic Bangui Windmills with eco-tourism events, kite-flying competitions, cultural showcases, and coastal activities along Bangui Bay.

November

Ilocos Norte Tourism Week

A week-long showcase of the province's best tourism offerings — farm tours, heritage walks, food festivals, and adventure activities for all ages.

December

Simbang Gabi & Paskuhan

The beloved nine-day dawn Mass tradition culminating on Christmas Eve — churches across the province light up with parol lanterns and Ilocano carols fill the air.
