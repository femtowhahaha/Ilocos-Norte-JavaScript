Ilocano Flavors

Food & Cuisine

Bold, fermented, and utterly distinct the cuisine of Ilocos Norte is one of the Philippines' most celebrated food cultures.

  1. Home
  2. Food & Cuisine
Must-Try Dishes

Signature Ilocano Dishes

Ilocano cuisine is defined by bold umami flavors, preserved meats, and the creative use of fermented condiments. Every dish tells a story of centuries of culinary tradition.

Bagnet

The pride of Ilocano cuisine double-fried pork belly that achieves a shattering crisp exterior while remaining succulent inside. Served with Ilocos vinegar or bagoong (fermented shrimp paste), bagnet is a culinary masterpiece of contrasting textures and bold flavors.

Ilocos Empanada

Unlike its Spanish cousin, the Ilocos empanada features a vivid orange rice-flour shell deep-fried to a satisfying crunch, filled with Ilocano longganisa sausage, green papaya, and egg. Available at every street corner in Laoag an unmissable snack.

Pinakbet

The quintessential Ilocano vegetable dish bitter melon, eggplant, squash, okra, and tomatoes slow-simmered with bagoong. Deeply savory, nutritious, and proudly representative of Ilocano cooking philosophy: honest, unpretentious, and deeply satisfying.

Ilocos Longganisa

Ilocos Norte's version of the classic Filipino sausage is garlicky, slightly sweet, and deeply savory. Smaller and denser than its Pampanga counterpart, the Ilocano longganisa pairs perfectly with vinegar and steamed rice for a classic Northern breakfast.

Dinengdeng

A simple but deeply nourishing Ilocano vegetable broth made with fish bagoong as the base, loaded with fresh vegetables and grilled fish. Humble in appearance but complex in flavor the definitive Ilocano home-cooked dish.

Poque-Poque

Grilled eggplant scrambled with eggs, tomatoes, and onions a simple, smoky, and utterly irresistible Ilocano dish. Often eaten for breakfast alongside longganisa, it captures the essence of Ilocano home cooking in every bite.

Where to Eat

Laoag Empanada Street

The legendary stretch along the Laoag Riverside Park where empanada vendors set up daily the most iconic street food experience in all of Ilocos Norte. Go before noon for the freshest batches.

Saramsam Ylocano Restaurant

One of the most celebrated authentic Ilocano restaurants in Laoag City bagnet, pinakbet, dinengdeng, and longganisa prepared in the traditional way. A must for any food traveler visiting the province.

Ilocos Norte Pasalubong Centers

Don't leave without Ilocos vinegar (sukang Iloko), longganisa, bagnet, and the famous Ilocos Royal Bibingka rice cakes made from glutinous rice and coconut. Available at pasalubong centers near the airport and downtown Laoag.

Best Times to Eat

Ilocos empanada is best in the morning (611 AM). Bagnet and sit-down restaurants serve lunch from 11 AM2 PM. For fresh seafood, head to Pagudpud's beachside restaurants in the early evening for the day's catch.
