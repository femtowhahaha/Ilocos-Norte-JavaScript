UNESCO World Heritage

Paoay Church

The Church of Saint Augustine in Paoay is one of four Baroque churches inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site under the collective designation "Baroque Churches of the Philippines" in 1993.

Built between 1694 and 1710, the church is a supreme example of Earthquake Baroque — a uniquely Filipino architectural style developed to withstand seismic activity. Its massive coral stone buttresses, designed to brace the structure against earthquakes, are architecturally unlike any building in Asia.

The church has survived centuries of earthquakes, wars, and the ravages of time. It was used as a watchtower during the Philippine Revolution and as a machine gun nest during World War II — its walls scarred with bullet marks that are now part of its storied identity.

Lighthouse · 1892

Cape Bojeador Lighthouse

Built in 1892 by the Spanish colonial government, this octagonal brick lighthouse stands on Burgos Hill overlooking the South China Sea. It is one of the tallest and best-preserved lighthouses in the Philippines, with panoramic views of the northern coast.

Church · Laoag

Saint William Cathedral

The main church of Laoag City, dedicated to Saint William the Hermit. Alongside it stands the iconic sinking bell tower — an 18th-century free-standing campanile that leans visibly due to unstable ground, a beloved Laoag landmark.

Learn More
Historic Site · Batac

Marcos Mausoleum

Located in Batac City, this site contains the refrigerated remains of former President Ferdinand Marcos. The accompanying museum documents Ilocos Norte's role in national politics and history — a significant and complex part of Philippine heritage.
