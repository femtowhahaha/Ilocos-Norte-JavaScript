The Province

About Ilocos Norte

At the northwestern tip of Luzon, where the South China Sea meets ancient Ilocano civilization.

Ilocos Norte is a province of extraordinary contrasts — where 400-year-old Spanish colonial churches stand amid landscapes of striking natural beauty. Bordered by the Luzon Strait to the north and the South China Sea to the west, this storied province has nurtured a resilient, proud people whose culture permeates every corner.

The province is home to the UNESCO-listed Paoay Church, the iconic Bangui Bay wind farm, and a culinary tradition as bold and complex as the Ilocano spirit itself. Its capital, Laoag City, pulses with life — a thriving center where ancient and modern coexist in elegant harmony.
