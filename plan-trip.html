Step by Step

Your Planning Checklist

1

Choose Your Season

November–May (dry season) is ideal for beaches and outdoor activities. February is peak festival month. Book early for Pamulinawen (Feb 10) as accommodations sell out weeks ahead.

2

Book Your Flight or Bus

Fly into Laoag International Airport (LAO) from Manila — ~1 hour. Or take an overnight bus from Cubao/Pasay (9–11 hours, ₱700–₱1,200). Book air tickets 2–4 weeks in advance for the best fares.

3

Reserve Accommodation

Stay in Laoag City for heritage & dune access. Stay in Pagudpud for beaches. Split your stay between both for the complete experience. Book beachfront resorts 1–2 months ahead for peak months.

4

Arrange Transport

Hire a tourist van with driver (₱2,500–₱4,000/day) for convenience, or use jeepneys and tricycles on a budget. Rent a motorcycle (₱400–₱800/day) for solo adventurers.

5

Build Your Itinerary

Day 1: Laoag City — La Paz Sand Dunes 4x4, empanada at the riverside, Saint William Cathedral & Sinking Bell Tower.
Day 2: Paoay Church (UNESCO), Bangui Windmills at sunset.
Day 3: Pagudpud — Saud Beach, Kabigan Falls trek, Blue Lagoon, Kapurpurawan, Patapat Viaduct.

6

Prepare & Pack

Essentials: lightweight clothing, SPF 50+ sunscreen, insect repellent, comfortable shoes, raincoat (wet season), power bank, sufficient cash (ATMs are limited outside Laoag), reusable water bottle.

7

Enjoy & Explore!

Stay flexible — the best Ilocos Norte moments are often unplanned. Try every local food you encounter, learn a few Ilocano phrases (Naimbag a bigat = Good morning!), and embrace the legendary Ilocano warmth and hospitality.

Day 3 — Saud Beach → Kabigan Falls → Blue Lagoon → Patapat Viaduct → Return to Laoag
