Bangui Windmills

Southeast Asia's first wind farm — 20 towering turbines rising from the black-sand shores of Bangui Bay.

Overview

About the Bangui Windmills

The Bangui Wind Farm is a 25-megawatt wind energy facility along the shores of Bangui Bay — and the first commercial wind farm in Southeast Asia, commencing operations in 2005. It stands as both a landmark of sustainable energy and one of the most visually striking tourist attractions in the entire Philippines.

The farm consists of 20 Vestas V82 wind turbines, each standing approximately 70 meters tall with 82-meter rotor blade spans. Arranged in a single row stretching 1.2 kilometers along the black-sand coast facing the South China Sea, they create a surreal, cinematic landscape unlike anywhere else in the Philippines.

Visitors can walk freely among the turbines and along the black-sand shoreline, feeling the powerful sea wind that powers both the turbines and the local imagination. At sunrise and sunset, the turbines cast long shadows across the dark sand — creating one of the most photographed scenes in Philippine travel photography.

Getting There

How to Reach Bangui

By Bus/Jeepney: Take a jeepney or Claveria-bound bus from Laoag terminal. Ask to be dropped at Bangui. ~1.5–2 hours. Fare ₱60–₱120.
By Private Van/Car: Drive north from Laoag via the national highway. ~80 km, approximately 1.5 hours. Free parking at the wind farm.
By Motorcycle: Rent in Laoag (₱400–₱800/day). A scenic 90-minute coastal ride northward. Ideal for adventurous independent travelers.

📸 Photography Tips

  • Best light: Golden hour (sunrise 5:30–6:30 AM, sunset 5:30–6:30 PM)
  • Wide-angle lens recommended
  • Walk the full 1.2 km length for varied angles
  • Include the black-sand beach in foreground
  • Drone flights require LTO/CAAP permit
Important Note

The wind turbines are operational energy infrastructure — do NOT climb or touch the turbines. Stay on the public beach access areas. Respect the energy facility boundaries.
