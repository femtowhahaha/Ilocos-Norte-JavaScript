Getting Here

How to Arrive

Three main ways to reach Ilocos Norte — by air, bus, or private car.

By Air ✈️

Laoag International Airport (LAO) serves daily flights from Manila via Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and AirAsia. Flight time is approximately 1 hour. The airport is located just 5 km from downtown Laoag. Taxis, tricycles, and hotel shuttles are available at the terminal.

Typical Airfare

₱1,500–₱4,500 one-way (Manila–Laoag, varies by airline and booking date)

By Bus 🚌

Comfortable air-conditioned buses depart daily from Manila's Cubao and Pasay terminals. Major operators: Partas, Farinas, GV Florida, Dominion. Travel time is 9–11 hours. Overnight buses are the most popular budget option — depart at night, arrive in Laoag by morning.

Typical Fare

₱700–₱1,200 one-way (regular to deluxe class)

By Car 🚗

Drive from Manila via NLEX → SCTEX → TPLEX → NLEx Rosario then north along the Ilocos coastal highway through Ilocos Sur. Total distance approximately 480 km. Travel time is 7–8 hours depending on traffic. Highly scenic coastal drive especially through Ilocos Sur.

Estimated Fuel

₱1,500–₱2,500 round trip depending on vehicle type

Getting Around

Local Transport

🛺

Tricycle

Primary short-distance transport within towns. Negotiate fare before boarding. Great for getting around Laoag, Bangui, and Pagudpud town centers.

₱10–200
per ride
🚌

Jeepney

Connects towns and municipalities across the province. Routes run from Laoag to Batac, Paoay, Bangui, and Pagudpud. Flag down at the roadside.

₱15–80
per trip
🚐

Tourist Van

Most convenient for tourists. Hire a private van with driver for customized itineraries. Hotels and tour agencies in Laoag can arrange bookings.

₱2,000–4,000
per day
🛵

Motorcycle Rental

Ideal for independent exploration. Rent motorcycles or scooters in Laoag City. Requires a valid Philippine or international license.

₱400–800
per day

Pro Tip

For maximum flexibility and comfort, hiring a tourist van with a local driver-guide for ₱2,500–₱3,500/day is highly recommended. A good local driver will know shortcuts, best photo spots, and where to eat.

Road Conditions

Main highways are well-paved and clearly signed. Some barangay roads to waterfalls and mountain areas may be rough — 4x4 vehicles are recommended for Adams municipality.

Taxi / Ride-Hailing

Grab operates in Laoag City with limited availability. Traditional taxi meters are rare — agree on a fixed price before boarding any unmarked taxi or tricycle for longer trips.
